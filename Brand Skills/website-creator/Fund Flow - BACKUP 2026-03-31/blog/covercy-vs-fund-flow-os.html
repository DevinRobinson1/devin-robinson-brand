What Is Covercy?

Covercy is a banking-embedded real estate investment management platform. Its defining feature is FDIC-insured LP checking accounts built directly inside the platform, allowing GPs to execute zero-fee ACH distributions to investors without leaving the software. Covercy also includes eSignatures, automated waterfalls, investor portals, K-1 matching, a fundraising pipeline, AI content generation for marketing copy, and Yardi/Rent Manager integrations. Pricing starts at $398/month for two legal entities and scales per entity added.

Covercy's banking-embedded architecture is the differentiator that no other investor management platform has matched. The idea is simple and powerful: instead of moving money out of your platform into a bank and then back into another system to process distributions, Covercy holds LP funds in FDIC-insured checking accounts within the platform itself. That eliminates a step that causes real operational friction for operators running multiple distributions per quarter.

The platform covers the full post-capital-raise workflow:

  • LP checking accounts: FDIC-insured accounts inside the platform where LP capital sits until deployed
  • Zero-fee ACH distributions: Distribute directly to investors without third-party payment processing fees
  • Investor portal: Clean LP-facing portal for position visibility, documents, and updates
  • Automated waterfalls: IRR calculations, preferred return tracking, and equity multiple distributions
  • K-1 matching: Automated tax document distribution at year-end
  • eSignatures: Subscription document signing built in
  • Fundraising pipeline: Interactive pitch deck and investor accreditation workflow
  • Yardi and Rent Manager integration: For operators already running property management software

Covercy's banking-embedded architecture keeps LP funds and distributions inside one platform, eliminating the need for separate banking integrations.

The entity-based pricing model is worth understanding before you get deep into evaluation. Covercy's Standard plan is $480/month for two legal entities. That works out to $240 per entity per month. If you manage four funds or SPVs, you're at $960/month. Seven entities puts you at $1,680/month or more. For operators running a single fund, that's manageable. For operators building a multi-entity structure, the math changes fast.

Covercy's AI content generator is worth naming accurately: it produces marketing copy and investor communication drafts. It is not an autonomous outreach agent. It does not send emails, track investor relationships, or log compliance evidence. That distinction matters a great deal when you're comparing platforms on the basis of "AI features."

The AI and Compliance Gap

Covercy's AI feature is a content generator for marketing copy and investor communications. It does not perform autonomous outreach, track investor relationship status, or create compliance documentation. Fund Flow OS includes Flow AI for autonomous outreach and Flow Guard for 506(b)/506(c) compliance tracking. For operators raising capital under a Regulation D exemption, the compliance gap between the two platforms is significant.

Let's be precise about what "AI" means in each platform, because the term is doing very different work in each case.

Covercy's AI content generator helps you write marketing copy, investor updates, and deal communications faster. It is a writing aid. You still decide who to contact, when to contact them, how to follow up, and what to say. The AI produces a draft. You do the work.

Fund Flow OS's Flow AI is an autonomous agent. It identifies who to reach out to based on relationship status and engagement signals, drafts the outreach in your voice, sends it, tracks the response, and logs everything automatically. It runs sequences across your entire pipeline simultaneously. That is not a writing tool. That is an employee replacement for a task that most operators are doing manually right now.

Flow AI runs autonomous outreach sequences across your entire investor pipeline. Covercy's AI generates content drafts. These are fundamentally different tools solving different problems.

The compliance gap is even more significant for operators raising under 506(b).

Under SEC Rule 506(b), you cannot use general solicitation, and you must demonstrate a pre-existing substantive relationship with each investor before presenting deal-specific information. That relationship must be documented. If you ever face an SEC inquiry, you need to show exactly when each touchpoint happened, what was communicated, and what stage the relationship was in when deal information was shared.

Covercy does not track any of this. There is no compliance layer, no relationship status tracking, and no documentation infrastructure for Regulation D purposes.

Flow Guard in Fund Flow OS was built specifically for this requirement. Every investor interaction is timestamped and logged. Relationship status is tracked with a Red/Yellow/Green system that reflects SEC relationship standards. Your compliance documentation is built automatically as you operate. Our co-founder Seth, an SEC attorney, designed the compliance architecture. The legal logic is baked into the product, not bolted on afterward.
