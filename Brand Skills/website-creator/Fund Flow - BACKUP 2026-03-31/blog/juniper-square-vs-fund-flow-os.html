What Is Juniper Square?

Juniper Square is an institutional partnership enablement platform for commercial real estate, private equity, and venture capital firms. Founded in 2014 and headquartered in San Francisco, it manages more than $300 billion in capital across its customer base. It provides an investor portal and CRM, fundraising tools, waterfall and distribution automation, fund administration services, and institutional-grade partnership accounting. Pricing is not publicly listed and requires a sales demo. The entry tier for firms under $50M equity is approximately $18,000 per year, with annual contract required.

Juniper Square is the platform that large institutional CRE operators trust. When a private equity firm managing $500 million in assets under management needs a platform that integrates with their fund administration team, produces institutional-grade reporting, handles complex waterfall structures, and manages hundreds of LP relationships at once, Juniper Square is a serious option.

The $300 billion in managed capital is not a marketing number. It reflects deep institutional adoption by firms that have the back-office infrastructure to get real value from an enterprise-grade platform.

Juniper Square's core capabilities are built for this world:

  • Institutional investor portal and CRM: Purpose-built for large LP relationships and fund-level reporting
  • Fundraising and investor relations tools: Managing capital calls, commitments, and investor communications at scale
  • Waterfall and distribution automation: Complex multi-tier waterfall structures handled natively
  • Fund administration services: Full-service back-office support including partnership accounting
  • Reporting and analytics: Institutional-grade performance reporting for LPs and GPs
  • Dedicated customer success: High-touch support appropriate for enterprise clients

Juniper Square is the institutional standard for firms managing hundreds of millions with dedicated back-office teams.

Where Juniper Square runs into friction is with operators who are earlier in their capital raising journey. The platform was not designed for someone closing their first 10 deals or raising their first $5M fund. It was designed for firms that already have $50M, $100M, or $500M under management and need enterprise infrastructure to match.

The pricing reflects this. Approximately $18,000 per year as the entry point is calibrated for institutional firms, not growth-stage operators. And that number does not include implementation fees, data migration costs, or the additional costs that show up below Enterprise tier for off-cloud backups and certain integrations.

Who Is Juniper Square Actually Built For?

Juniper Square is built for institutional CRE, private equity, and venture capital firms that are already managing large capital bases with full back-office teams. Its ideal customer is a firm with $50M to $500M or more in assets under management that needs enterprise-grade fund accounting, complex waterfall structures, and institutional LP reporting infrastructure. It is not designed for operators at the growth stage who are building from their first raise toward their first $100M.

Saying this clearly is not a knock on Juniper Square. It is honesty about what the platform is actually designed to do.

Juniper Square built a platform for a specific customer: a CRE or PE firm with a team, existing institutional LPs, a CFO or fund administrator in the loop, and complex accounting requirements that need software infrastructure to match. Their $300 billion in managed capital tells you exactly who that customer is.

That customer benefits from Juniper Square's partnership accounting, institutional reporting, dedicated customer success, and deep ecosystem integrations. The $18,000-per-year price point is reasonable relative to the operational complexity it addresses.

The friction shows up when operators who are not yet at that scale try to fit Juniper Square into their operation. If you are managing your first fund or scaling from private lending toward a structured fund, you are not the customer Juniper Square optimized for. You will pay for infrastructure you cannot yet leverage, lock into an annual commitment before you have proven the model, and miss the AI and compliance capabilities that matter most at your current stage.

Understanding that distinction upfront saves you a bad platform decision that costs you $18,000 and six months to unwind.
