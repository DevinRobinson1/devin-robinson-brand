What Is Agora?

Agora (agorareal.com) is a VC-backed institutional real estate investment management platform headquartered in New York and Tel Aviv. The company has raised $64 million and reports $25B+ in assets managed across its platform. Agora offers investor portals, CRM, fundraising suites, waterfall automation, K-1 management, and institutional data rooms. Its entry tier, Essential, starts at $749/month billed annually with no monthly option available.

Agora is the kind of platform that gets built when institutional money backs it. The $64 million in VC funding shows, and the $25B+ in managed assets is not a marketing number — that's real validation from real firms running real capital on the platform.

The platform covers the full institutional fund management stack:

  • Investor portal: Best-in-class LP self-service portal with document access, position visibility, and updates
  • Investor CRM: Robust contact management with fundraising suite and smart questionnaires
  • Waterfall automation: Full waterfall calculation engine including advanced structures at Pro tier
  • K-1 and document management: Tax document generation and distribution management
  • Report builder: Institutional report formats for LP reporting
  • Integrated payments: Free embedded ACH at Pro tier
  • Institutional data room: Secure LP due diligence repository (Pro tier and above)
  • Open-end fund accounting: NAV calculations for open-end structures (Pro tier and above)
  • SSO and enterprise integrations: Enterprise tier
  • Dedicated success manager: Included at Essential and above

Agora is built for institutional fund management, with $25B+ in managed assets across its platform.

Agora's pricing structure reflects its institutional positioning. The Essential tier at $749/month is the entry point, billed quarterly on an annual contract with a 30-day cancellation notice requirement. Pro and Enterprise are custom pricing. There is no monthly option and no free tier at any level.

For a firm that already has $20M+ AUM and is running formal fund structures, Agora makes a lot of sense. The platform is mature, institutional-grade, and backed by real capital. The problem is that most operators researching Agora are not yet at that stage, and committing $8,988 per year before you've scaled your capital base is a significant bet on your own growth timeline.
